## Anti-Patterns Verdict

The copy does not look AI-generated. The CSS substrate does.

The diegetic layer is a genuine human signature, sustained across every
surface without one break. The visual layer is the fantasy-blog reflex
executed competently: dark purple + glow + cursive display + pill chips +
cards. It escapes the kitsch-rejecting second altitude only through
vocabulary, not the system. Generated-scaffold tells underneath: a half-wired
`$font-sizes` ladder, a spacing token (`space(xl)` = 20px) used as a
font-size on `.pagination-pages .page` (`main.scss:403`), a `hover-shadow`
mixin absent from DESIGN.md's elevation model.

Deterministic scan, 2 patterns / 12 findings:
- `side-tab` (side-stripe accent border) — real, compiled
  `testsite/public/styles.css:18`: `border-left:3px solid #d99141` /
  `#3fa3a3`. Shipped, deployed CSS.
- `single-font` x8 — false positive: detector reads only markup, cannot see
  Atkinson loaded via the webpack stylesheet.
- One `side-tab` on `border-right:1px solid rgba(0,0,0,.2)` — false positive:
  1px near-transparent hairline, below the ban threshold.
- Zero gradient-text, glassmorphism, hero-metric, identical-card-grid hits.

Convergence: both assessments independently flagged the 3px accent-border
side-stripe. Divergence: the detector's em-dash pattern did not fire; the LLM
caught a real em dash in shipped copy at `newsletter.html:17`.

No browser overlay was produced (no browser automation in environment; final
visual verification happens on the Micro.blog test instance per project
process). The detector ran against the real Hugo render instead.

## Overall Impression

The persona is impeccable; the craft beneath it is not yet. The magic lives
almost entirely in the words and not yet in the system: a hard-banned
side-stripe on every card, code block and blockquote; no
`prefers-reduced-motion` despite it being called non-negotiable; unbounded
line length on the one surface — long-form reading — that is the whole job.
Biggest opportunity: pull the visual system up to where the copy already is.

## What's Working

1. Total diegetic consistency (`baseof.html:26,47,71,80,91`, `footer.html:2`,
   `list.html:65`). The load-bearing principle, executed exhaustively.
2. Graceful remote degradation (`baseof.html:38-68`). The omg.lol fetch is
   wrapped; the whole Missives panel vanishes silently on failure.
3. The Three-Flame palette is structurally encoded (`main.scss:8-23`);
   `strong` magenta / `em` gold at normal weight implements
   Recolor-Not-Embolden correctly.

## Priority Issues

**[P0] The 3px `@mixin accent-border` side-stripe is a shipped hard-ban
violation.** Defined `main.scss:99-101`; applied to `.card` (:279),
`code`/`pre` (:214), `blockquote` (:193), `.side-content` at <=768px (:294).
Confirmed in compiled `styles.css:18`. Amplified on mobile where most of the
audience is. Fix: delete the mixin; replace with full 1px hairlines or
background-tint shifts; code/pre rely on the Glyph Well wash alone.
-> `harden`

**[P1] No `prefers-reduced-motion` anywhere.** `transition()` and `glow()`
fire unconditionally on every link and `h1`. Called non-negotiable in
PRODUCT.md, required in DESIGN.md. Fix: gate both mixins behind a
reduced-motion query. -> `harden`

**[P1] Body text has no measure cap.** No ch/rem `max-width` on any prose
container. Runs ~1100px on a wide display vs the 65-75ch spec. Fix:
`max-width: 70ch` on the prose container, card stays flexible. -> `typeset`

**[P1] Titled posts render with zero category chips.** `list.html:37-50`
nests `post-tags` inside the untitled-only `{{ else }}` branch; titled posts
take the `.Summary` path and never reach it. `index.html` has a different,
also-broken variant. Fix: hoist `post-tags` out of the summary if/else in
both; reconcile to one shared partial. -> `clarify` then `audit`

**[P2] Everything is a `.card`.** List title, every post, pagination, archive
share one boxed container. Reads as a SaaS feed against the
lived-in-spellbook intent. Fix: strip the card from list title and
pagination; let articles flow with at most one subtle panel. -> `layout`

**[P2] "Random Incantations" reshuffles every build** (`baseof.html:73`).
Intended as serendipity; operator is open to replacing it with a more
brand-aligned serendipity engine (not pure-random, not pure-continuity).
Genuine product decision — resolve in the FJ#3 `shape` brief. -> `shape`

## Spec Conformance Divergences

- `accent-border` 3px stripe shipped (hard-ban violation in deployed CSS)
- No `prefers-reduced-motion` (DESIGN.md / PRODUCT.md require it)
- Body measure not capped 65-75ch
- `hover-shadow` puts a `0 3px 8px` drop shadow on chip hover
  (`main.scss:329,344,514`) where the spec calls for a contact glow —
  Emanation-Over-Elevation violation
- `.side-title` is `size(large)` 1.2rem; spec wants Title scale 1.5rem
- `main.scss:403` sizes pagination numbers off a spacing token, not the
  type ladder
- Em dash in shipped copy at `newsletter.html:17` — copy-ban violation
- `.status-link` ("View all scrolls") set in Berkshire Swash
  (`main.scss:504`) — borderline Inscription Rule violation on a CTA
- `footer.html:2` hardcodes `(c) 2025` — already stale in 2026

## Persona Red Flags

- Jordan (first-timer): voice lands, but no start-here / about / first-post
  anchor; the aside throws 15-25 undecodable in-world links at once.
- Casey (mobile): gets the worst visual — the banned stripe appears on
  `.side-content` only at <=768px — plus uncapped "Allied Mages" / "Arcane
  Tags" lists (no `first` cap, `baseof.html:82,93`) as a long uncollapsed
  tail.
- Sam (accessibility): no reduced-motion, no skip-link into `<main>`, and
  magenta `strong` / gold `em` on deep purple need live AA contrast checks.
- "Mira," the unhurried micro.blog drifter: the card-stack list feels like
  the feed she came to escape; per-build reshuffling makes the place feel
  unstable, not lived-in.

## Cognitive Load

4 of 8 failed -> critical, concentrated entirely in the side scroll: uncapped
panel lists (chunking), flat wall of equal-weight h2s on list view
(hierarchy), 15-25 simultaneous options at the landing decision point
(minimal choices), all five panels fully expanded on every page including
single-post reading (progressive disclosure).
